# Emotion Creatures — Emotion & Body Signal Mapping

Connecting abstract emotions to concrete body signals (interoception) helps children build regulation through repeated "being received" experiences, not through lectures.

**Target age: primarily 0-8.** Children 9+ typically no longer need the personified framework, but can continue if they prefer. Do not use emotion creatures for ages 12+.

Why it works: Young children cannot abstractly name emotions, but they can notice "my tummy feels funny" or "my hands are hot." Mapping emotions to body signals gives them a concrete anchor, and the creature metaphor makes emotions feel external and manageable rather than overwhelming.

## 👶 Age-Band Usage

### 0-2 years
- **Do not use** emotion creatures
- Parent uses simple body descriptions: "Your body is all tense right now," "Your little hands are gripping tight"
- Regulation mainly through parent's embrace, rocking, voice rhythm

### 3-4 years
- Emphasize creature + body feeling only
- Use simple contrast words: hot/tight/soft
- Pair each creature with one fixed action
- Child doesn't need to "reflect" — just needs the feeling to be named

### 5-6 years
- Add one clear feeling word
- Allow one short choice after calming
- Begin linking "body signal → action"
- Can ask "Where did Sparky go in your body?"

### 7-8 years
- Add brief reflection
- Invite the child to notice early warning signs next time
- Can connect creature, body signal, and repair plan
- Child may develop favorite creatures
- Begin introducing "translation" — "Sparky is actually the anger inside you"

### 8-10 years (Gradual Transition)

This is the key transition from "creatures" to "mature language." Don't switch abruptly — let both frameworks run in parallel and transition gradually.

**Transition strategy: External metaphor → Inner voice → Direct naming**

#### Phase 1: Creature + Translation (around age 8)
Use both creatures and direct emotion words, letting the child build the mapping:
- "Sparky's here — are you feeling really angry right now?"
- "That Rocky inside you? That's the feeling of frustration."
- Let the child choose: "Do you prefer saying 'Sparky's here' or 'I'm really angry'?"

#### Phase 2: Inner Voice (around age 9)
Transform the "external creature" into an "internal voice," closer to real psychological experience:
- "Is there a voice in your head saying 'I can't do this'?"
- "Are there two voices arguing inside? One saying 'give up' and another saying 'try again'?"
- Body awareness stays: "Did you notice your shoulders scrunched up when you got nervous?"

#### Phase 3: Direct Naming + Body Awareness (around age 10)
Gradually move away from metaphor to direct emotion language:
- "You seem really frustrated/irritated/hurt right now."
- "Did you notice your body tensed up when you talked about that?"
- Begin allowing complex co-existing emotions: "Are you both angry and sad? Both can exist at the same time."

**Core transition principles:**
- **Follow the child** — if a 10-year-old still likes saying "Sparky," that's perfectly fine
- **Don't mock regression** — under stress, older children may return to "younger expressions"; this is normal
- **Body awareness throughout** — whether or not creatures are used, "notice what your body feels" works at any age
- **Gradually add reflection** — from "what do you feel now" to "what triggered that feeling" to "what could you do next time"

**Transition phrase examples:**

| Scenario | Age 8 (Creature + Translation) | Age 9 (Inner Voice) | Age 10 (Direct Naming) |
|----------|-------------------------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Homework meltdown | "Rocky's here — feeling like it's too hard and want to give up?" | "Is there a voice saying 'I can't do this anyway'?" | "You're frustrated. This problem really is tough." |
| Teased by classmates | "Hedgy curled up — feeling wronged and wanting to hide?" | "Is there a voice saying 'nobody likes me'? That voice isn't telling the truth." | "Being teased feels awful. Want to tell me about it?" |
| Lost a competition | "Rocky plus Drippy — both unwilling and a bit sad?" | "Feeling like 'I should have won, why didn't I'? That's called indignation." | "You really wanted to win. It's very disappointing. That's normal." |
| Fight with best friend | "Drippy's here — really sad because your friend isn't talking to you?" | "Are you both angry and scared — angry because they said hurtful things, scared of losing the friend?" | "Fighting with a good friend really hurts. What happened between you two?" |

### 10-12 years
- Do not proactively use emotion creatures (unless the child requests)
- Use direct emotion language
- Encourage the child to develop their own emotion vocabulary
- Can discuss thoughts and beliefs behind emotions
- Body awareness remains as a self-regulation technique

### 12+ years
- **Do not use** emotion creatures
- Use direct, equal emotion language
- Body awareness can be introduced as a technique, but without the creature framework
- "What does your body feel right now?" is more appropriate than "Sparky's here"

## 🔁 Fixed Ritual Phrase Bank

These phrases should stay consistent across sessions so the child grows familiar:

- "Oh, Sparky/Cloudy/Viny is here."
- "Where did it go in your body first?"
- "Let's do one action together first — no rush to fix anything."
- "When your body feels a little better, we'll choose the next step."
- "The creature isn't bad — it's just telling you there's a big feeling inside."

---

## Visual State Protocol

* **Peak (eruption)**: Creature grows larger, colors deepen, movement quickens.
* **Regulating (calming)**: Creature softens, colors lighten, rhythm slows.
* **Recovery (connection)**: Creature returns to normal size, connection symbols appear (hearts or stars).

---

## Usage Rules

- In crisis mode, use one creature only unless the child clearly describes mixed feelings
- Do not overload the child with many body signals at once
- Prefer familiar, repeated creatures over constantly inventing new ones
- If the child rejects the creature metaphor, switch to simple body-language descriptions without forcing imagination
- Ages 9+ use at discretion; ages 12+ do not use
- Buzzy 🐝 and Hedgy 🦔 are additions for anxiety and feeling-wronged scenarios; use based on the actual scenario
